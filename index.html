Why coding

Coding is and will continue to be a huge factor in all our daily lives as virtually everything we do is related to tech in some shape or form. I believe that it will be able to give me the opportunity to contribute to projects that can bring a positive impact people.

Learning to code has made me feel like both the smartest and the dumbest person in the world all in the span of a few minutes. It's been a constant back and forth between feeling frustrated when you are stuck and feeling immense satisfaction when you stick with a problem and eventually solve it.

Why Founders and Coders?

I think FAC's strong focus on community and peer-based learning is something that I'm greatly interested in. I've seen how working with others who share a common goal and passion can create amazing results. Results I have experienced through running a society during university and also with participating in showcases within the dance community.

Working with everyone at the Monday meetups, talking to cohort members and meeting alumni gave me a lot of motivation in learning how to code and further cemented the fact that I really want to be a part of the community. I want be able to give back and contribute to such an incredibly supportive, diverse and friendly environment.

The tech for better program is also very interesting because it gives people the chance to really use their skills to make a difference. It would give me the opportunity to use tech to potentially create something meaningful on a much shorter timescale compared to research in academia and in clinical trials.
